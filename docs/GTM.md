# Go-To-Market — LevelUp Kids

The plan for getting LevelUp Kids in front of the right ten families during the 6-week build, the right hundred during v1.1, and the right thousand by the end of Q3.

The `gtm-innovation` agent reads this every run. When it writes tickets that touch the landing page, the referral surface, the email copy, or the parent-portal share moments, this is the playbook it pulls from.

## 1. The thesis

There is no "parenting Duolingo" today. Existing players win on a *single* axis (a learning app, a chore app, a calendar app) and leave the family-development problem un-owned. The wedge is the **Family Growth Score** — the one number a parent screenshots and shares with their best friend. If we get that surface right, growth is the loop.

## 2. The ideal first family

Not "every parent." Specifically:

- **Two parents, ages 32–44**, dual-income, urban or affluent suburban.
- **1–3 kids, ages 6–11.** Old enough for the loop to make sense; young enough that habits actually take.
- **Already paying for one of:** Duolingo Family, Khan Academy Kids, GoNoodle, a martial-arts dojo, a tutor. They've voted with their wallet that "we are intentional about who our kids become."
- **Faith-positive household** — character and purpose pillars resonate. We do not pick a specific tradition (the app speaks to Muslim, Christian, Jewish, Buddhist, secular-virtuous families alike), but we lean into virtue as the moat against "another screen-time app."
- **Active in 1+ parent community** — a school WhatsApp, a mosque/church/temple parent circle, a Facebook group, a "moms of {neighborhood}" Slack.

We are NOT trying to win the cost-conscious, "is this free" segment in year one. We are building for the segment that pays.

## 3. Positioning

### One-line

> "LevelUp Kids is the family OS that helps you raise the kind of adult you actually want to raise."

### Three-line

> Parents have one number that matters and no app that tracks it: are we actually raising the kids we said we would raise?
>
> LevelUp Kids turns the eight dimensions of a thriving child — Scholar, Athlete, Builder, Creator, Leader, Character, Explorer, Purpose — into a daily Quest → XP → Level loop and a single Family Growth Score.
>
> It's Duolingo for raising kids. Built for the family that's already intentional.

### Anti-positioning (what we are NOT)

- Not a chore app. We don't pay kids to do dishes.
- Not a screen-time tracker. We make screens earn their keep.
- Not a school replacement. School is fine; we're the *rest* of the day.
- Not a "kid app." We're a *family* app — the parent is the lead user.

## 4. The launch sequence

### Weeks 1–6 (the build) — 10 design-partner families

- **Goal:** ship v1.0; observe 10 families using it daily; collect three "show me" screenshots that prove the moment.
- **Sourcing the 10:** founder's own circle. Targeted asks of 30 friends-of-friends in the persona. No paid; no landing-page traffic yet.
- **Onboarding:** founder personally onboards each family on a 25-minute call. Watches them complete signup → first-quest → first-approval on a real device. Times it with a stopwatch — the success criterion.
- **Asks:**
  1. "Send me a screenshot the first time your kid leveled up."
  2. "Send me one screenshot of your parent dashboard, end of week 1."
  3. "Would you pay $9.99/month for this today? Why or why not?"
- **Output:** three testimonials, three screenshots, a price-sensitivity read.

### Weeks 7–14 (v1.1 + first 100 families) — soft launch

- **Goal:** AI Family Coach + Achievements + Stripe live. 100 families on Premium. $1K MRR.
- **Channels (ranked by leverage):**
  1. **Founder's network referrals.** The 10 design-partner families each refer 2 — that's 20. The other 80 come from the founder's own LinkedIn, X, and community circles. Targeted ask: "I built a thing. Here's the screenshot. If this looks useful for your family, here's the link — first month free."
  2. **One long-form essay.** Published on the founder's personal blog and cross-posted to one Substack and one X thread. Title direction: *"What we're actually measuring when we say we want our kids to be 'good people.'"* The essay is the moat — it's the position paper that makes the product make sense.
  3. **Two parent-circle drops.** Pick two specific high-trust parent communities (one faith-based, one neighborhood-school). Founder posts once, with permission, with a video walkthrough.
- **Pricing:**
  - **Free:** 1 household, up to 3 children, daily + weekly quests, basic Family Growth Score.
  - **Premium ($9.99/month):** unlimited children, AI Family Coach, advanced analytics, seasonal campaigns (Ramadan, Lent, summer-break).
  - **Annual:** $84/year (30% discount) — the only annual plan; no lifetime, no early-bird.
- **Anti-channels:** App Store paid search, Meta ads, influencers, TikTok virality plays. None of these in 2026; we're building the wedge, not buying users.

### Weeks 15+ (the moat) — to 1,000 families

- **Goal:** 1,000 paying households by end of Q3 2026. The Family Growth Score becomes the share artifact.
- **Channels:**
  1. **Family Growth Score share card.** v1.1 ticket: a parent can share an *anonymized* weekly recap to one friend, one tap. The card is the screenshot. The CTA on the card is "see your family's score."
  2. **Co-parent invite is the viral loop.** Half of v1.0 households have one parent on the app; the v1.1 "invite your co-parent" surface gets them both. Each adds friction to leaving.
  3. **Faith-tradition seasonal campaigns.** Ramadan in v1.1, Lent in v1.2, summer-break in v1.3. Each is a "21-day family challenge" landing page seeded into the relevant community.
  4. **Founder-led content.** One essay per month. Three short videos a week (parent dashboard tour, week-in-the-life, "what we learned from 100 families"). Not influencer; founder.

## 5. The five "show me" moments

A user shares a product when there's a single screenshot they want to send. v1.0 + v1.1 must produce these five:

1. **The first level-up.** Child reaches Level 1. Parent screenshots. The composition (avatar + level pill + the quest that did it) is the share card.
2. **The Family Growth Score radar.** End of week 2. The 8-axis chart, with the two focus pillars filled in, sits cleanly on a phone screen.
3. **The approval queue.** Parent taps approve, three rows collapse in a satisfying motion. (Demo'd as a 5-second loop on the landing page.)
4. **The streak.** Layla, day 14. Confident, calm, no confetti.
5. **The AI Family Coach answer** (v1.1). A parent asks "How do I help Yusuf get into reading?" — the coach answers with a tailored 3-day mini-mission. Screenshot is the share.

Every v1.0 + v1.1 ticket that touches the surface should ask: *which of these five moments does this improve?* If the answer is none, it's the wrong ticket.

## 6. The landing page (week 6)

One page. No carousel. No "Why us." No founder-letter wall.

```
[hero]
  warm photo of a parent + child looking at the parent's phone, both relaxed
  H1 (Fraunces): "Raise the kind of adult you actually want to raise."
  Sub:           "LevelUp Kids turns the eight dimensions of a thriving child
                  into a daily quest, a level, and one Family Growth Score —
                  for the family that's already intentional."
  CTA:           "Start with your family"  (no ", free!" — the funnel sells trust, not freemium)

[the loop]
  three screenshots, side-by-side, each captioned in one sentence:
    1. Child opens app, sees today's quests
    2. Child completes a quest, taps "I did it"
    3. Parent approves, XP awarded, level rises

[the eight pillars]
  the eight cards, each with its pillar icon, name, one-sentence "what this means
  in practice." not a list — a tile.

[the moat]
  the Family Growth Score radar chart, with one paragraph:
    "The score is the one number a parent will screenshot at the end of every
     month — proof, not promise, that the kid you said you wanted to raise is
     the kid you're actually raising."

[founder note]  (≤ 200 words)
[CTA]           "Start with your family"
```

That's it. Ticket lives in the v1.0 backlog as "0020 — landing page" if the build moves faster than expected; otherwise lands in v1.1.

## 7. The metrics that matter

We do NOT optimize for DAU or MAU in 2026.

| Metric | Why | Target by end of Q3 2026 |
|---|---|---|
| **Family-week retention** = % of households with ≥ 1 approved completion in week N+1 of joining | Proves the loop holds | ≥ 60% W1, ≥ 40% W4 |
| **Co-parent activation** = % of households with 2 active parents | Proves the family layer matters | ≥ 50% |
| **Score-share rate** = % of households who tap "share" on the weekly recap (v1.1) | The viral coefficient | ≥ 15% by month 3 |
| **Free → Premium conversion** | The business | ≥ 8% by month 3 |
| **Monthly churn** | The moat-vs-novelty test | ≤ 5% |

Lagging:
- **Monthly recurring revenue.** $1K by month 2, $10K by month 6.
- **Net Promoter Score** at month 2 of each household. ≥ 40.

## 8. The competitive read

| Competitor | What they do | Where we win |
|---|---|---|
| **Greenlight, BusyKid** | Chore apps with allowance | We're not money. We're virtue. |
| **Duolingo Kids** | One subject (language) | We're whole-child. The Family Growth Score doesn't exist there. |
| **Habitica, Streaks** | Adult habit trackers | Adult-shaped UI. We're parent + child shaped. |
| **Cozi, Apple Family** | Logistics calendar | We're development, not logistics. |
| **Khan Academy Kids** | Academic content | Academic only. We're 8 pillars. |
| **Headspace for Kids** | One pillar (mindfulness) | Same shape; we own all 8. |

The flank: nobody owns the *whole-child, family-scoped, gamified-daily-loop* + *Family Growth Score* surface. That's our category.

## 9. The risks and how we handle them

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Parents install, kids don't engage | Ticket: child onboarding tour after the parent finishes setup. Use the child's name in every greeting. v1.0 ships this. |
| Parents engage week 1, churn week 4 | The Family Growth Score weekly recap email (v1.1). It's the comeback hook. |
| Pricing feels rich for "an app" | Founder's note on the landing page reframes it as "less than a single tutoring session, every month." Plus the annual at $84 lowers the daily mental cost. |
| Faith-positive copy alienates secular families | The eight pillars *include* `character` and `purpose` but never name a specific religion. The copy says "what does honesty look like in your family this week," not "as a Christian/Muslim/Jewish family." v1.1 adds optional tradition-specific seasonal campaigns as add-ons; v1.0 stays universal. |
| AI Coach hallucinates a bad parenting suggestion | The v1.1 ticket constrains the coach with a system prompt that names the eight pillars, age ranges, and "never suggest anything that requires the child to be alone with a stranger, leave home unattended, or violate the parent's stated values." Plus human review on every coach output for the first 100 families. |
| Apple in-app-purchase 30% tax | We ship as a PWA, billed by Stripe, on the web. No app store. We accept the install-friction tradeoff. |

## 10. What the gtm-innovation agent should write tickets about

When this agent grooms or ideates, it picks from these veins, in priority order:

1. **A "show me" moment that doesn't exist yet.** Above all else. If one of the five moments is half-built, finish it before adding a new surface.
2. **A co-parent activation surface.** Every household with one active parent is a churn risk. Every household with two is a moat.
3. **A friction cut in the first-10-minute onboarding.** A second saved here is a parent retained.
4. **A weekly recap that the parent forwards to one specific person.** Not a generic email; a specific moment.
5. **A pricing surface that names what the parent gets, not what the tier is called.** "Unlimited children" beats "Premium."

The agent does NOT write tickets about:
- Landing-page carousels.
- "AI for everything" surfaces.
- Notification opt-ins on first launch (we wait until day 3).
- Onboarding tutorials longer than three steps.
